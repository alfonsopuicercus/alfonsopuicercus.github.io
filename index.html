What I work on

Particle physics experiments need detectors that can tell apart two particles arriving mere picoseconds apart — and do it millions of times per second. My work is about pushing silicon sensors closer to that limit.

I run TCAD simulations to model the electric fields and charge collection inside 3D silicon sensors, then feed those results into Garfield++ to simulate how a real particle would move through the device and what signal it would leave behind. Together, these tools let me predict the spatial and time resolution of a sensor before it's ever manufactured.

I'm doing this as part of the LHCb experiment at CERN, where a new vertex detector is being designed that will need to track particles with better timing than anything that's been built before. The goal is to understand which sensor geometries and configurations get us there.
